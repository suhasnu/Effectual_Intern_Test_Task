# Analysis of Structural Change (2000-2025)

**Which countries show signs of de-industrialization?**
Analysis of the collected data reveals noticeable marks of de-industrialization in several advanced economies like Australia, Canada, Belgium, and France. In these countries, the share of total industry value added and even more so the share of manufacturing value added, have dropped significantly relative to total GDP over the considered period.

**Is manufacturing declining faster than total industry?**
Yes, in these advanced economies, manufacturing is usually falling faster than the wider industry sector. For example, the manufacturing sectors in Canada and Italy experienced negative compound annual growth rates (CAGR) during the period, contracting in absolute terms while the total industry was relatively stagnant or growing slightly.

**Are trends different between advanced and emerging economies?**
Emerging economies have a very different structural trajectory to that of advanced economies. China's industrial share has remained strong and stable at above 37% with great GDP growth, indicating a long period of industrialisation and not a move away from manufacturing into services. Indeed, Turkiye’s share of manufacturing increased from 15.4% to 16.3%. This reflects a clear divergence in industrial trends between advanced and emerging markets.
